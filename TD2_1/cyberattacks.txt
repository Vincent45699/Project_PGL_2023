					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
A <a href="/wiki/Cyberattack" title="Cyberattack">cyberattack</a> is any type of offensive maneuver employed by individuals or whole organizations that targets computer information systems, infrastructures, computer networks, and/or personal computer devices by various means of malicious acts usually originating from an anonymous source that either steals, alters, or destroys a specified target by hacking into a susceptible system.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indiscriminate_attacks">Indiscriminate attacks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Indiscriminate attacks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These attacks are wide-ranging, global and do not seem to discriminate among governments and companies.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Operation_Shady_RAT" title="Operation Shady RAT">Operation Shady RAT</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/World_of_Hell" title="World of Hell">World of Hell</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Red_October_(malware)" title="Red October (malware)">Red October</a>, discovered in 2012, was reportedly operating worldwide for up to five years prior to discovery, transmitting information ranging from diplomatic secrets to personal information, including from mobile devices.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/WannaCry_ransomware_attack" title="WannaCry ransomware attack">WannaCry ransomware attack</a> on 12 May 2017 affecting hundreds of thousands of computers in more than 150 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2017_Petya_cyberattack" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 Petya cyberattack">2017 Petya cyberattack</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destructive_attacks">Destructive attacks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destructive attacks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These attacks relate to inflicting damage on specific organizations.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Great_Hacker_War" title="Great Hacker War">Great Hacker War</a>, and purported "gang war" in <a href="/wiki/Cyberspace" title="Cyberspace">cyberspace</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LulzRaft" title="LulzRaft">LulzRaft</a>, hacker group known for a low impact attack in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Operation_Ababil" title="Operation Ababil">Operation Ababil</a>, conducted against <a href="/wiki/Banking_in_the_United_States" title="Banking in the United States">American financial institutions</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/TV5Monde#April_2015_Cyberattack_and_resulting_disruption" title="TV5Monde">TV5Monde April 2015 cyberattack</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vulcanbot" title="Vulcanbot">Vulcanbot</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shamoon" title="Shamoon">Shamoon</a>, a modular computer virus, was used in 2012 in an attack on 30,000 <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Aramco" title="Saudi Aramco">Saudi Aramco</a> workstations, causing the company to spend a week restoring their services.<sup id="cite_ref-Iran_Malware_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iran_Malware-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wiper_(malware)" title="Wiper (malware)">Wiper</a> – in December 2011, the malware successfully erased information on <a href="/wiki/Hard_disk_drive" title="Hard disk drive">hard disks</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Energy,_Industry_and_Mineral_Resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Energy, Industry and Mineral Resources">Oil Ministry's</a> headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stuxnet" title="Stuxnet">Stuxnet</a> - A <a href="/wiki/Malware" title="Malware">malicious</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_worm" title="Computer worm">computer worm</a> believed to be a jointly built <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a>-<a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> <a href="/wiki/Cyber_weapon" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyber weapon">cyber weapon</a>.   Designed to sabotage <a href="/wiki/Iran%27s_nuclear_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Iran&#39;s nuclear program">Iran's nuclear program</a> with what would seem like a long series of unfortunate accidents.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cyberwarfare">Cyberwarfare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cyberwarfare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Cyberwarfare" title="Cyberwarfare">Cyberwarfare</a></div>
<p>These are politically motivated destructive attacks aimed at sabotage and espionage.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2007_cyberattacks_on_Estonia" title="2007 cyberattacks on Estonia">2007 cyberattacks on Estonia</a>, wide-ranging attack targeting government and commercial institutions</li>
<li>2008 <a href="/wiki/Cyberattacks_during_the_Russo-Georgian_War" title="Cyberattacks during the Russo-Georgian War">Cyberattacks during the Russo-Georgian War</a>, a series of cyberattacks that swamped and disabled websites of numerous South Ossetian, Georgian, Russian and Azerbaijani organizations. The attacks were initiated three weeks before the shooting war began in what is regarded as "the first case in history of a coordinated cyberspace domain attack synchronized with major combat actions in the other warfighting domains (consisting of Land, Air, Sea, and Space)."</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2009_DDoS_attacks_against_South_Korea" title="2009 DDoS attacks against South Korea">2009 DDoS attacks against South Korea</a>, a series of coordinated cyberattacks against major government, news media, and financial websites in South Korea and the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_2009_cyberattacks" class="mw-redirect" title="July 2009 cyberattacks">July 2009 cyberattacks</a>, against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and the United States</li>
<li>2009 <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Network" title="Shadow Network">Shadow Network</a>,  China-based computer espionage operation that stole classified documents and emails from the Indian government, the office of the Dalai Lama, and other high-level government networks.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2010_Australian_cyberattacks" title="February 2010 Australian cyberattacks">2010 Australian cyberattacks</a>, a series of denial-of-service attacks conducted by the Anonymous online community against the Australian government in response to proposed web censorship regulations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010_cyberattacks_on_Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 cyberattacks on Burma">2010 cyberattacks on Burma</a>, related to the <a href="/wiki/2010_Myanmar_general_election" title="2010 Myanmar general election">2010 Myanmar general election</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010_cyberattacks_on_Myanmar" title="2010 cyberattacks on Myanmar">2010 cyberattacks on Myanmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denial-of-service_attack#Distributed_attack" title="Denial-of-service attack">distributed</a> <a href="/wiki/Denial-of-service_attack" title="Denial-of-service attack">denial-of-service attacks</a> (DDoS) ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Myanmar_general_election" title="2010 Myanmar general election">2010 Myanmar general election</a>, which is widely viewed as a <a href="/wiki/Sham_election" class="mw-redirect" title="Sham election">sham election</a>.</li>
<li>2010 <a href="/wiki/Operation_Olympic_Games" title="Operation Olympic Games">Operation Olympic Games</a>, against Iranian nuclear facilities, allegedly conducted by the United States</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010_Japan%E2%80%93South_Korea_cyberwarfare" title="2010 Japan–South Korea cyberwarfare">2010 Japan–South Korea cyberwarfare</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_government_hackings" title="2011 Canadian government hackings">2011 Canadian government hackings</a>, hackers using IP addresses from China infiltrated 3 departments within the government and exfiltrated classified data. The attacks resulted in the government cutting off internet access in the departments affected and various responses from both the Canadian government and the Chinese government.</li>
<li>2012 <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ababil" title="Operation Ababil">Operation Ababil</a>, a series of cyber attacks starting in 2012, targeting various American financial institutions and carried out by a group calling itself the Cyber fighters of Izz Ad-Din Al Qassam.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2013_Singapore_cyberattacks" title="2013 Singapore cyberattacks">2013 Singapore cyberattacks</a>, attack by <a href="/wiki/Anonymous_(group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Anonymous (group)">Anonymous</a> "in response to <a href="/wiki/Internet_censorship_in_Singapore" title="Internet censorship in Singapore">web censorship regulations in the country</a>, specifically on <a href="/wiki/News_media" title="News media">news outlets</a>"</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2013_South_Korea_cyberattack" title="2013 South Korea cyberattack">2013 South Korea cyberattack</a>, two major sets of cyberattacks on South Korean targets attributed to elements within North Korea.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Office_of_Personnel_Management_data_breach" title="Office of Personnel Management data breach">Office of Personnel Management data breach</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2015_Ukraine_power_grid_hack" title="2015 Ukraine power grid hack">2015 Ukraine power grid hack</a>, took place during an ongoing conflict in Ukraine and is attributed to a Russian advanced persistent threat group known as "Sandworm". It is the first publicly acknowledged successful cyberattack on a power grid.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2016_Kyiv_cyberattack" title="2016 Kyiv cyberattack">2016 Kyiv cyberattack</a>, which caused another power outage</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Committee_cyber_attacks" title="Democratic National Committee cyber attacks">Democratic National Committee cyber attacks</a>, against the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Committee" title="Democratic National Committee">Democratic National Committee</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a>-sponsored <a href="/wiki/Cyber_spying" title="Cyber spying">cyber-espionage</a> groups <a href="/wiki/Cozy_Bear" title="Cozy Bear">Cozy Bear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fancy_Bear" title="Fancy Bear">Fancy Bear</a>, possibly to assist <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump's</a> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump_2016_presidential_campaign" title="Donald Trump 2016 presidential campaign">2016 presidential campaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2017_cyberattacks_on_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 cyberattacks on Ukraine">2017 cyberattacks on Ukraine</a>, A series of powerful cyberattacks using the Petya malware began on 27 June 2017 that swamped websites of Ukrainian organizations, including banks, ministries, newspapers and electricity firms. Similar infections were reported in France, Germany, Italy, Poland, Russia, United Kingdom, the United States and Australia.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2019_cyberattacks_on_Sri_Lanka" title="2019 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka">2019 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka</a>, The 2019 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka were a series of powerful cyberattacks on at least 10 Sri Lankan domestic websites with the public domains of .lk and .com.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2020_cyberattacks_on_Sri_Lanka" title="2020 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka">2020 cyberattacks on Sri Lanka</a>, a series of cyberattacks on at least 5 Sri Lankan national websites with the top-level domains of .gov and .com.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2021_Cyberattacks_on_Sri_Lanka" class="mw-redirect" title="2021 Cyberattacks on Sri Lanka">2021 Cyberattacks on Sri Lanka</a>,  series of cyberattacks on at least 10 Sri Lankan national websites including Google.lk domain</li>

<li>Between July 7, 2021, to July 14, 2021, the Indian government email infrastructure was compromised thrice with hackers accessing emails of several top officials including that of Ajay Prakash Sawhney, the secretary to the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Electronics_and_Information_Technology" title="Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology">Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stolen_credit_card_and_financial_data">Stolen credit card and financial data</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Stolen credit card and financial data">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2017_Equifax_data_breach" title="2017 Equifax data breach">2017 Equifax data breach</a> - In 2017, <a href="/wiki/Equifax" title="Equifax">Equifax Inc</a>. announced that a cyber-security breach occurred between May to mid July of that year. <a href="/wiki/Cyber_criminals" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyber criminals">Cyber criminals</a> had accessed approximately 145.5 million U.S. Equifax consumers' personal data, including their full names, <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_number" title="Social Security number">Social Security numbers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Credit_card" title="Credit card">credit card</a> information, <a href="/wiki/Birthday" title="Birthday">birth dates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Address" title="Address">addresses</a>, and, in some cases, <a href="/wiki/Driver%27s_license" title="Driver&#39;s license">driver's license</a> numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2016_Indian_Banks_data_breach" title="2016 Indian Banks data breach">2016 Indian Banks data breach</a> - It was estimated 3.2 million <a href="/wiki/Debit_card" title="Debit card">debit cards</a> were compromised. Major <a href="/wiki/Banking_in_India" title="Banking in India">Indian banks</a>- <a href="/wiki/State_Bank_of_India" title="State Bank of India">SBI</a>, <a href="/wiki/HDFC_Bank" title="HDFC Bank">HDFC Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/ICICI" class="mw-redirect" title="ICICI">ICICI</a>, <a href="/wiki/YES_Bank" class="mw-redirect" title="YES Bank">YES Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Axis_Bank" title="Axis Bank">Axis Bank</a> were among the worst hit.<sup id="cite_ref-et-20oct2016_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-et-20oct2016-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Industries" title="Goodwill Industries">Goodwill Industries</a> – in September 2014, the company suffered from a credit card data breach that affected the charitable retailer's stores in at least 21 states. Another two retailers were affected.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Home_Depot" class="mw-redirect" title="Home Depot">Home Depot</a> – in September 2014, the cybercriminals that compromised Home Depot's network and installed malware on the home-supply company's <a href="/wiki/Point_of_sale" title="Point of sale">point-of-sale systems</a> likely stole information on 56 million <a href="/wiki/Payment_card" title="Payment card">payment cards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>StarDust – in 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Botnet" title="Botnet">botnet</a> compromised 20,000 cards in active campaign hitting US merchants.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a> – in 2013, approximately 40 million credit and debit card accounts were impacted in a credit card breach.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> According to another estimate, it compromised as many as 110 million Target customers.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Visa_Inc." title="Visa Inc.">Visa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a> – in 2012, they warned card-issuing banks that a third-party payments processor suffered a security breach, affecting up to 10 million credit cards.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Subway_(restaurant)" title="Subway (restaurant)">Subway</a> – in 2012, two <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> men admitted to participating in an international conspiracy that hacked into credit-card payment terminals at more than 150 Subway <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">restaurant franchises</a> and stole data for more than 146,000 accounts.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/MasterCard" class="mw-redirect" title="MasterCard">MasterCard</a> – in 2005, the company announced that up to 40 million cardholders may have had account information stolen due to one of its payment processors being hacked.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Blockchain_and_cryptocurrencies">Blockchain and cryptocurrencies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_cyberattacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Blockchain and cryptocurrencies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li>The Uncle Maker attack - an attack on <a href="/wiki/Ethereum" title="Ethereum">Ethereum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/F2Pool" class="mw-redirect" title="F2Pool">F2Pool</a> <a href="/wiki/Mining_pool" title="Mining pool">mining pool</a>, which lasted between 2020 and 2022, but was only discovered in 2022 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aviv_Yaish&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aviv Yaish (page does not exist)">Aviv Yaish</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilad_Stern&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gilad Stern (page does not exist)">Gilad Stern</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aviv_Zohar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aviv Zohar (page does not exist)">Aviv Zohar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>BNB Chain hack - in early October 2022, about $570 million in cryptocurrency was stolen from a bridge for the BNB Chain, a blockchain operated by the <a href="/wiki/Binance" title="Binance">Binance</a> exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Because a majority of the tokens could not be transferred off-chain, the hacker ultimately made off with about $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
